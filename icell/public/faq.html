The University Explore Program helps students identify the right universities based on their GPA, test scores, career goals, and budget. It includes shortlisting, profile evaluation, and personalised counselling sessions with our experts.
You can register for multiple programs. For example, you can enrol in the IELTS coaching program while simultaneously working on university shortlisting through the University Explore Program.
We cover 15+ countries including the USA, Canada, UK, Australia, Germany, Netherlands, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, and more. Our counsellors have specialized expertise in each destination.
Yes, 100% free for all LBRCE students. The IELTS coaching program covers all four modules — Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking — and includes 12 full-length mock tests and one-on-one feedback sessions.
The standard program runs for 8 weeks with 3 sessions per week. Intensive 4-week crash courses are also available for students with upcoming exam dates.
Yes, we offer GRE and GMAT preparation workshops. These are conducted as 6-week modules with study materials, practice tests, and expert guidance from our test prep team.
Requirements vary by country and university. Generally, USA and Australia require a minimum of 6.5 overall, UK requires 6.0–7.0, and Canada requires 6.5–7.0. Our counsellors provide destination-specific guidance.
Standard documents include: valid passport, university acceptance letter, financial proof (bank statements/sponsorship), IELTS/TOEFL scorecard, academic transcripts, Statement of Purpose, and visa application form. Our team provides a country-specific checklist at your counselling session.
Processing times vary: USA (F-1) – 4–8 weeks, Canada – 4–12 weeks, UK – 3–4 weeks, Australia – 4–6 weeks. We recommend applying at least 3 months before your program start date.
We maintain a 98% visa success rate across all countries, achieved by thorough document review, SOP guidance, and mock visa interviews conducted before students submit their applications.
Yes! Many universities offer merit-based scholarships for international students. We also guide students toward government scholarships like Fulbright, Commonwealth, DAAD, and Chevening, as well as university-specific grants and assistantships.
Most merit scholarships require a GPA of 8.0+ (on a 10-point scale) or equivalent. Research assistantships are more accessible and often accompany MS admissions at research-focused universities.
